## Betting Rationale In Detail

### Expected-Value Rule

The strategy chooses the result and exact score with the largest modeled MPG point expectation:

```text
base expected points = probability of selected result x MPG result points
exact-score bonus EV = probability of selected exact score x bonus points
total expected points = base expected points + exact-score bonus EV
```

The selected side need not be the outcome with the largest raw probability. MPG gives more points for less obvious results, so the relevant comparison is probability multiplied by points, plus the value of an exact-score selection.

### Score Selection Pattern

Most recommendations use `2-1` or `1-2`. These scorelines preserve a meaningful win probability while often qualifying for a higher exact-score bonus than the most concentrated low-variance outcome. Stronger favorites such as Germany, Spain, Austria, and Portugal shift toward `2-0`; Turkey and Ecuador are recommended at `0-1`.

### Decision Risk

Expected points are averages across repeated comparable situations, not a predicted round score. Close decisions such as South Korea over Czechia and Ecuador over the draw carry greater model sensitivity. Strong edges reduce the chance that small odds updates alter the optimal strategy, but they do not eliminate match outcome variance.
